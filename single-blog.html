Problems are everywhere. Defining a problem is the primary and the hardest step for any individual who wants to solve a certain problem. The reason behind this is that it requires the diagnosis of the situation to focus on the real problem and not the symptoms of that problem. Many people might argue on the point that sometimes identifying or finding the problem is more important than the solution.

For example, Galileo recognized the problem of needing to know the speed of light but did not come up with a solution. It took advances in mathematics and science to solve this measurement problem. Yet Galileo still received credit for finding the problem. Sometimes problem definition may be nothing more than the art of asking the right questions at the right time[1].

Problems are approached and solved in different ways depending on people or a certain type of problem. Entrepreneurs solve problems because entrepreneurs see problems as opportunities, while other people might solve problems to continue living. Knowing that problems are different means that there is no single approach to solve problems, but when we recognize the real problem most of the time we gain the ability to solve that problem.

Inside the mind of every problem solver, there is always a cycle of how he can approach a problem to solve it. The Act, Learn, Build cycle is the frequently used cycle to address a lot of problems.
